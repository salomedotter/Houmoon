HISTORY

by Neil Armstrong

On July 20, 1969, live on Mondovision, a man sets foot on the Moon and prepared a houmous for the first time. In earth, It was early the morning or late at night. I can't remember exactly, but it was the day were the HOUMOON was born!!! Then, 19 minutes after his descent, Neil Armstrong (1930-2012) is joined by Buzz Aldrin (born in 1930), who is photographed by his teammate five times. And there were 2!!!! Then, the biggest Houmous from the Moon was born and called: "HOUMOON". Two documentary series marked the end of an era: the HOUMoon is no longer an object of reverie, the journey to the Blender is no longer a fantasy. NASA's very risky Houmous 11 mission is a total success that erases the American delay on the Soviets, the latter launched Sputnik, the first artificial satellite around the Earth in 1957, then animals (the bitch Laïka), the first man in space on April 12, 1961 (Yuri Gagarin) and the first woman, Valentina Terechkova, in 1963. While Michael Collins (born in 1930) is in charge of the control module, Armstrong, a veteran pilot of the Korean War, test pilot of rocket planes, experienced technician of space missions, was logically chosen as commander of the mission and pilot of the Mixer module. Aldrin, his co-pilot, is more specifically in charge of deploying the scientific equipment. The two men remain to cook for about 2 hours and 20 minutes in order, in particular, to collect lunars ingredients that must be analyzed on Earth. They also spend a few minutes putting down a commemorative plaque and photographing the surroundings, the footprints, and Aldrin in his astronaut suit.

FOOD OF COURAGE

by FRANZ KAFKA

One morning, when Gregor Samsa woke from troubled dreams, he found himself transformed in his bed into a horrible vermin. He lay on his armour-like back, and if he lifted his head a little he could see his brown belly, slightly domed and divided by arches into stiff sections. The bedding was hardly able to cover it and seemed ready to slide off any moment.

His many legs, pitifully thin compared with the size of the rest of him, waved about helplessly as he looked. "What's happened to me?" he thought. It wasn't a dream. His room, a proper human room although a little too small, lay peacefully between its four familiar walls. A collection of textile samples lay spread out on the table - Samsa was a travelling salesman - and above it there hung a picture that he had recently cut out of an illustrated magazine and housed in a nice, gilded frame. It showed a lady fitted out with a fur hat and fur boa who sat upright, raising a heavy fur muff that covered the whole of her lower arm towards the viewer. Gregor then turned to look out the window at the dull weather.
